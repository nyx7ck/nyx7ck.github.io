For what values of h and k does the linear system have infinitely many solutions?

2x1 + 5x2 =  − 1

hx1 + kx2 = 3







If the linear system has infinitely many solutions, it has to have at least one free variable.

Transform from linear system to matrix form:

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 2 & 5 & -1 \\ h & k & 3 \end{array}\right]\)

By applying Row Operation: \[R_2 - \frac{h}{2}R_1 \rightarrow R_2\] we get:

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 2 & 5 & -1 \\ 0 & k - \frac{5}{2}h & \frac{h}{2} + 3 \end{array}\right]\)

In order to have infinitely many solutions, we have to satisfy two equations below:

$$k - \frac{5}{2}h = 0$$ and $$\frac{h}{2} + 3 = 0$$

Solving the second equation, we can get: $$\frac{h}{2} = -3$$

h =  − 6

Substitute h =  − 6 into the first equation and solve for k: $$k - \frac{5}{2}h = 0$$

$$k + \frac{5}{2}(6) = 0$$

k + 15 = 0

k =  − 15

Thus, the only solution could make this linear system inconsistent is:

{h, k ∈ ℝ  |  h =  − 6  &  k =  − 15}
