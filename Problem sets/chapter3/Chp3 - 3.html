Determine if the given function is a linear transformation.
\[ T(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix}) = \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos{x_2} \\ 3\sin{x_3} \\ x_1 \end{bmatrix} \]






A linear transformation is closed in both vector addition and scalar multiplication.

Assume \[\vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} u_1\\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{bmatrix}, \quad \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v _1\\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix} \] Let's check vector addition: \[ T(\vec{u}) + T(\vec{v}) = \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos{u_2} \\ 3\sin{u_3} \\ u_1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos{v_2} \\ 3\sin{v_3} \\ v_1 \end{bmatrix} \]
\[ T(\vec{u}) + T(\vec{v}) = \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos{u_2} + 2\cos{v_2} \\ 3\sin{u_3} + 3\sin{v_3} \\ u_1 + v_1 \end{bmatrix} \neq \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos{(u_2 + v_2)} \\ 3\sin{(u_3 + v_3)} \\ u_1 + v_1 \end{bmatrix} \]
\[ T(\vec{u}) + T(\vec{v}) \neq T(\vec{u} + \vec{v}) \]
Vector addition is not closed!

Thus, \(T(\vec{x})\) is not a linear transformation.
