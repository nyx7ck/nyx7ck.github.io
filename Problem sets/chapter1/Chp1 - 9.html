Find the values of the coefficients a, b, and c for \[f(x) = ae^x + be^{2x} + ce^{-3x}\] so the given conditions are met.

f (0) = 2

f ′(0) = 1

f ″(0) = 19







1. Solve for the f ′(x) and f ″(x)

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = ae^x + 2be^{2x} -3ce^{-3x}$$

$$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = ae^x + 4be^{2x} + 9ce^{-3x}$$

2. Substitute three conditions into three equations we got:

2 = a + b + c

1 = a + 2b − 3c

19 = a + 4b + 9c

3. Turning this linear system to matrix form:

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 1 & 4 & 9 & 19 \end{array}\right]\)

4. Applying Elementary Row Operations to get REF:

\[\xrightarrow[R_3 - R_1 \rightarrow R_3]{R_2 - R_1 \rightarrow R_2}\]

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -1 \\ 0 & 3 & 8 & 17 \end{array}\right]\)

\[\xrightarrow{R_3 - 3R_2 \rightarrow R_3}\]

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 20 & 20 \end{array}\right]\)

3. Applying the backward substitution:

c = 1

b − 4c =  − 1

a + b + c = 2

Solving these equations, we get:

$$\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$$ Thus, \[ f(x) = -2e^x + 3e^{2x} + e^{-3x} \]
