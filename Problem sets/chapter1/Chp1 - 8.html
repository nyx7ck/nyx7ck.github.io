Figure below shows the plot of the points (1,4), (2,7), and (3,14).

Find a polynomial of degree 2 of the form f(x) = ax2 + bx + c whose graph passes through these points.







1. Substitute three points into f(x), so we get:

4 = a + b + c

7 = 4a + 2b + c

14 = 9a + 3b + c

2. Turning this linear system to matrix form:

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 4 & 2 & 1 & 7 \\ 9 & 3 & 1 & 14 \end{array}\right]\)

3. Applying Elementary Row Operations to get REF:

\[\xrightarrow[R_3 - 9R_1 \rightarrow R_3]{R_2 - 4R_1 \rightarrow R_2}\]

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & -2 & -3 & -9 \\ 0 & -6 & -8 & -22 \end{array}\right]\)

\[\xrightarrow{R_3 - 3R_2 \rightarrow R_3}\]

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & -2 & -3 & -9 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 5 \end{array}\right]\)

3. Applying the backward substitution:

c = 5

 − 2b − 3c =  − 9

a + b + c = 4

Solving these equations, we get:

$$\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}$$

Thus, \[ f(x) = 2x^2 - 3x + 5\]
