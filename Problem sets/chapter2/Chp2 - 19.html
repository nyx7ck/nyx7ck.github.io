If A is a matrix with columns that Span Rn, then \( A\vec{x} = \vec{b} \) has a solution for all b in Rn







1. By reading the question carefully, notice:

\[ \#columns \geq \#rows \]

so the shapes of the matrix would either be horizontal chunk or square matrix :

$$\begin{bmatrix} * & * & * \\ * & * & * \end{bmatrix} \quad or \quad \begin{bmatrix} * & * \\ * & * \end{bmatrix}$$



2. The question mention that the column vectors span Rn. In other words, doing RREF to this matrix will have a matrix like these:

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \quad or \quad \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$



3. We proceed proof by contradiction: assuming A is a matrix with columns that span Rn, then \( A\vec{x} = \vec{b}\) has no solution for all b in Rn. If we could find one counterexample, prove the assuming statement is false, then the original statement will be true!

Notice:

\[I\vec{x} = \vec{b}\]


$$\left[ \begin{array} {cc|c} 1 & 0 & b_1 \\ 0 & 1 & b_2 \end{array} \right]$$


$$\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix}$$

Column vectors of Identity matrix did span R2, and applying back-substitution, there is a unique solution, so our assumption is false.

Thus, the statement is True.
