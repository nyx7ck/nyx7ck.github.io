Let \(T(\vec{x}) = A\vec{x}\) for the given matrix A.

Determine if T is one-to-one and if T is onto.

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 9 & 6\\ \end{bmatrix} \]






The direct way is observing pivots' position for the RREF(or REF) of the Matrix \(A\)

Here is the RREF of the Matrix A: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \frac{2}{3}\\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Not Onto because these column vectors cannot span \(R^2\).

Not One-to-One because the second column vector is linearly dependent with the first column vector.

Thus, this linear transformation neither onto nor one-to-one.
