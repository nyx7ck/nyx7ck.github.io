For what value(s) of k is the linear system consistent?

6x1 − 5x2 = 4

9x1 + kx2 =  − 1







If the linear system has one or infinitely many solutions, it is consistent.


Transform from linear system to matrix form:

\( \left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 6 & -5 & 4 \\ 9 & k & -1 \end{array} \right] \)

By applying Elementary Row Operation: 2R2 − 3R1 → R2, we get:

\( \left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 6 & -5 & 4 \\ 0 & 2k + 15 & -14 \end{array} \right] \)

In order to be consistent, the second pivot couldn’t be zero, so we have:

2k + 15 ≠ 0

2k ≠ −15

$$k \neq -\frac{15}{2}$$

So, values of k could be:

$$\{k \in \mathbb{R}| k \neq -\frac{15}{2}\}$$
