The given matrix equation is not true in general. Explain why. Assume that all matrices are \(n \times n\) \[ A^2 - B^2 = (A - B)(A + B) \]






Let's verify from the right side of the matrix equation: \[ (A - B)(A + B) = A^2 + AB - BA - B^2 \]

The statement is false because \(AB\) do not always equal to \(BA\), so \(AB - BA = 0\) does not always hold true.
