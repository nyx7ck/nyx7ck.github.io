For what value(s) of h is the linear system consistent?

6x1 − 8x2 = h

 − 9x1 + 12x2 =  − 1







If the linear system has one or infinitely many solutions, it is consistent.

Transform from linear system to matrix form:

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 6 & -8 & h \\ -9 & 12 & -1 \end{array}\right]\)

By applying Row Operation: 3R1 + 2R2 → R2, we get:

\(\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 6 & -8 & h \\ 0 & 0 & 3h-2 \end{array} \right]\)

In order to be consistent, the second pivot has to be zero, so we have:

3h − 2 = 0

3h = 2

$$h = \frac{2}{3}$$

So, values of h could be:

$$\{h \in \mathbb{R}| h = \frac{2}{3}\}$$
