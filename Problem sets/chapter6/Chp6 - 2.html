Use the characteristic polynomial to determine if \(\lambda \) is an eigenvalue for the matrix \(A\) . \[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 3 & -1\\ -4 & -1 & 1\\ 18 & 6 & -4\\ \end{bmatrix}, \quad \lambda = 1 \]






If 1 is the correct eigenvalue, then \(\det(A - \lambda I)\) will be equal to zero

\[ \det(A - \lambda I) = \det(\begin{bmatrix} 6 - 1 & 3 & -1\\ -4 & -1 - 1 & 1\\ 18 & 6 & -4 - 1\\ \end{bmatrix}) \]
\[ \det(\begin{bmatrix} 6 - 1 & 3 & -1\\ -4 & -1 - 1 & 1\\ 18 & 6 & -4 - 1\\ \end{bmatrix}) = \det(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 & -1\\ -4 & -2 & 1\\ 18 & 6 & -5\\ \end{bmatrix}) \]
\[ \det(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 & -1\\ -4 & -2 & 1\\ 18 & 6 & -5\\ \end{bmatrix}) = -\det(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 3 & 5\\ 1 & -2 & -4\\ -5 & 6 & 18\\ \end{bmatrix}) \]
\[ -\det(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 3 & 5\\ 1 & -2 & -4\\ -5 & 6 & 18\\ \end{bmatrix}) = -\det(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 3 & 5\\ 0 & 1 & 1\\ 0 & -9 & -7\\ \end{bmatrix}) \]
\[ -\det(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 3 & 5\\ 0 & 1 & 1\\ 0 & 0 & 2\\ \end{bmatrix}) = -[(-1)(1)(2)] = 2 \neq 0 \]
Thus, 1 is not the eigenvalue for the matrix \(A\)
