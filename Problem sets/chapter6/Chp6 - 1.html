Use the characteristic polynomial to determine if \(\lambda \) is an eigenvalue for the matrix \(A\) . \[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 7 \\ -1 & 6 \\ \end{bmatrix}, \quad \lambda = 3 \]






If 3 is the correct eigenvalue, then \(\det(A - \lambda I)\) will be equal to zero

\[ \det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - 3 & 7 \\ -1 & 6 - 3 \\ \end{bmatrix} \]
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 - 3 & 7 \\ -1 & 6 - 3 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 7 \\ -1 & 3 \\ \end{bmatrix} = -3 + 7 = 4 \neq 0 \]
Thus, 3 is not the eigenvalue for the matrix \(A\)
